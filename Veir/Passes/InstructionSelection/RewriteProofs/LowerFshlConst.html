    Correctness of fshlConst_local #

    llvm.intr.fshl a a (const) with identical data operands and a constant shift amount is a constant rotate-left. There is no roli/roliw, so (like LLVM) a rotate-left by amt is emitted as a rotate-right by width - amt: on i64 it is lowered to unrealized_conversion_cast (value → register) → riscv.rori (immediate form, negated amount) → unrealized_conversion_cast (back to i64); on i32 the exact roriw shape of roliw_local (negated immediate).

    This is the width-generic sibling of roliw_local: everything up to and including the value cast is shared; the lowering then branches on bitwidth = 32 (emitting roriw, whose refinement is fshl_roriw_const_isRefinedBy_toInt) vs the i64 case (emitting rori, refined by fshl_rori_const_isRefinedBy_toInt). Both data-level refinements are discharged directly by veir_bv_decide after normalizing the (negated) immediate to the low bits of the constant.
