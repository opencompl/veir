    Generic correctness of udivPow2GenLocal #

    udivPow2GenLocal dst h width strength-reduces udiv x (2^k) to a logical shift right by k (riscv.srli/srliw): castToReg x → dst (k) → castBack. Instances: udivPow2 (srli, i64), udivwPow2 (srliw, i32).

    Same castToReg → imm-op → castBack shape as selectSingleBitLocal, with the emitted immediate the shift amount k recovered from matchUnsignedPow2Divisor (and matchUdiv/.udiv baked into the def). The two new data ingredients are udiv_two_pow_eq_shift (x / 2^k = x >>> k on bitvectors, proved via toNat since bv_decide times out on division) and mupd_singleSetBit (a successful power-of-two divisor match is a single set bit, reusing singleSetBit_bridge). The source udiv raises UB on a zero/poison divisor, so the hSemSrc discharge is the UB-aware split (as in udiv_local).

    Unsigned division by a single-bit power of two is a logical shift right. bv_decide times out on the 64-bit division circuit with a variable shift, so this goes through toNat.
