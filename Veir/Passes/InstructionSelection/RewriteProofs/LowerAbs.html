    Correctness of abs_local #

    llvm.intr.abs on a 64-bit integer is lowered to unrealized_conversion_cast (to a register) → riscv.neg → riscv.max (of the original register and its negation) → unrealized_conversion_cast (back to the integer type), mirroring LLVM's RV64+Zbb neg/max expansion of abs.

    abs does not fit lowerUnaryWLocal: it is i64-only and emits two register ops whose second (max) is a binary op consuming both the cast register and the neg result. It is therefore proven bespoke; structurally it is the lowerUnaryWLocal/bswap i64 shape with an extra interpretOp_riscv_unaryReg_forward (neg) step and a interpretOp_riscv_binaryReg_forward (max) step in place of the single unary-register forward step.
