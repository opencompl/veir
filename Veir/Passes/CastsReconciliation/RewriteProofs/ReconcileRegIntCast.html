    Correctness of reconcileRegIntCastLocal #

    reg -> iX -> reg keeps only the low X bits of the register: Reg.toInt truncates and Int.toReg zero-extends back, and no poison is ever involved (Reg.toInt is never poison). The rewrite replaces the round trip by the RISC-V instruction that computes that zero-extension: zext.b/zext.h/zext.w at X = 8/16/32, and an slli/srli pair by 64 - X otherwise.

    X = 0 is excluded by the pattern: the round trip is then the constant zero, while the shift pair would wrap its 6-bit shift amount 64 - 0 back to 0 and return the register unchanged.
